narrative: |
  [Taula d'entitats del Tercer Sector de Catalunya](https://www.tercersector.cat) brings together hundreds of organisations working across Catalonia in social services, housing, inclusion, and community support. As the sector's umbrella body, Taula holds a unique position: they aggregate data that reflects the real state of civil society in Catalonia, and they have a mandate to make that data useful to the organisations, researchers, and policymakers who depend on it.

  Until 2025, that data had no public home. Datasets were held internally with no structured way to publish, navigate, or share them. Policymakers couldn't find what they needed. Member organisations couldn't explore sector trends. The data existed — it just wasn't accessible.

  [OneTandem](https://www.onetandem.com/), a Catalan data and digital product studio, was brought in to solve this. While evaluating open data portal options for the project, they discovered [PortalJS Cloud](https://cloud.portaljs.com) and reached out to Datopian. After a brief evaluation, the decision was straightforward: a managed, customisable platform that could be live quickly, with no infrastructure overhead for the client.

  The critical requirement was taxonomy. Catalan third-sector data isn't organised by generic topic — it's structured around *àmbits* (thematic domains: housing, health, education, employment) and *col·lectius* (population groups: elderly, youth, migrants, people at risk of exclusion). No off-the-shelf portal reflected this. Datopian built it as real navigation logic, so the portal speaks the sector's language natively.

  [Espai de Dades](https://espaidedades.tercersector.cat/) launched with 139 datasets, fully in Catalan, with the Taula team managing it independently from day one.
